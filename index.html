Key Figures

The People Behind the Mission

Main Character · Mathematician

Katherine Johnson

played by Taraji P. Henson

Katherine is an extraordinary mathematician with an almost supernatural ability to perform complex trajectory calculations in her head. As the first Black woman to rise through the ranks to NASA's research division, she must break down invisible walls of racism and sexism every day — without ever losing her calm demeanor.

precise steadfast persevering humble curious courageous

Behavior & Characteristics

She tackles obstacles in silence — she proves her competence through performance, not confrontation.
She courageously asks questions in meetings where women were never supposed to be present.
Walks kilometers every day to reach the only "colored" restroom — without complaining, until she finally speaks up.
Has a deep, unshakable conviction: numbers don't lie — and she knows how to read them.

"I am a fully-fledged researcher — and a fully-fledged woman. Just like everyone else."

Main Character · Computer Scientist

Dorothy Vaughan

played by Octavia Spencer

Dorothy is the unofficial supervisor of the Black female computers — a leader who never received the official title she deserved. Pragmatic, forward-thinking, and determined, she recognizes early on the future of computing technology and independently teaches herself and her entire team what NASA will soon need most.

strategic forward-thinking loyal pragmatic composed self-determined

Behavior & Characteristics

Independently learns FORTRAN from a library book — simply takes it when she is denied access to the section.
Protects her team with quiet resolve and ensures everyone is brought along when opportunities open up.
Confronts injustice directly but with control — using facts rather than emotion.
Makes herself indispensable before anyone realizes she is missing.

"I don't have this job yet — but I've been doing it for a long time. Isn't that proof enough?"

Main Character · Engineer

Mary Jackson

played by Janelle Monáe

Mary is the most vivacious of the three — bold, self-assured, and fearless. As NASA's first Black female engineer, she fights for her degree through a court order. She does not believe in shrinking herself to fit in. Instead, she opens doors — for herself and for everyone who comes after her.

fearless charismatic direct ambitious witty combative

Behavior & Characteristics

Goes to court to gain access to engineering courses at an all-white school — and wins.
Says what others only think — directly, sharply, without apology.
Encourages Katherine to speak up, and always pushes herself one step further.
Wears heels to work in the wind tunnel — style as a statement, not a question.

"Every day I have to be the first at something. Every single day."

Director · NASA Task Group Head

Al Harrison

played by Kevin Costner

Al Harrison is the head of the Space Task Group — a man whose sole obsession is putting the first American into orbit. Racism doesn't interest him as a matter of principle — he is too busy trying to beat the Soviet Union. Paradoxically, this pragmatic indifference makes him an unexpected ally for Katherine.

results-driven impatient fair pragmatic protective

Behavior & Characteristics

Personally tears down the "Colored Women" restroom sign — not as a gesture, but because it costs him productive time.
Brings Katherine into briefing meetings because quality, to him, has no skin color.
Treats Katherine with respect without actively fighting the discrimination around him — so long as it doesn't interfere with his mission.
Slowly learns to see the full human being behind the mathematician.

"Here at NASA, we all pee the same color."

Astronaut · First American in Orbit

John Glenn

played by Glen Powell

John Glenn is the charming, down-to-earth astronaut who completes the first crewed Earth orbit for the United States. He is a genuine hero — but his most remarkable quality is his ability to listen. He trusts Katherine Johnson unconditionally and insists that she personally verify the NASA computer's calculations before his flight. For him, competence is competence — regardless of race or gender.

trusting respectful grounded brave open-minded

Behavior & Characteristics

Insists on Katherine personally checking the calculations before launch — and calls her by name.
Greets the Black female computers warmly and respectfully — a rare exception in his time.
Shows nerves but refuses to be stopped by them — he flies anyway.
Publicly credits Katherine in an environment that routinely denies her visibility.

"If the lady says the numbers are good, I want to hear it from her directly."

Colleague · Antagonist

Paul Stafford

played by Jim Parsons

Paul Stafford is Katherine's direct supervisor in the Space Task Group — a brilliant engineer guided by both unconscious and very conscious bias. He sees Katherine as a threat to his status, not as a colleague. His racism is rarely loud — it shows itself in small humiliations, in withheld credit, and in systematic obstacles he presents as perfectly normal.

status-conscious defensive biased arrogant competent passive-aggressive

Behavior & Characteristics

Redacts Katherine's name from reports — her contribution must remain invisible.
Bars Katherine from entering the briefing room on the grounds that it is "not customary" for women.
Delegates tasks to Katherine but never officially acknowledges her performance.
Never appears overtly hostile — he smiles while holding the doors shut.

"That's not how we do things here." — A sentence that has locked more doors than any key ever could.
